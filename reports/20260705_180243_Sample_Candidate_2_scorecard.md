## Risk flags

- **Mission alignment is unverified and undemonstrated.** This is the most consequential flag. Palantir explicitly hires people who have *chosen* its mission in defense intelligence and enterprise AI — and screens out candidates who treat that mission as background noise. Nothing in Priya's profile signals active interest in Palantir's government work, defense technology, or national security posture. At peer companies like Anduril, generic answers on mission are disqualifying. This must be probed directly and early.

- **Relocation from San Francisco to New York is a live variable.** She is listed as "open to NYC relocation" — but open is not the same as committed. Given Palantir's compensation range ($90K–$120K base) is meaningfully below the NYC technical recruiter market midpoint of $123K–$146K, the financial reality of relocating from SF to NYC on this comp may create friction or a short-tenure risk if a competing offer emerges locally.

- **Analytics and forecasting depth is unconfirmed.** Palantir is a data company — and this role requires comfort with pipeline metrics and forecasting, not just hitting targets. Priya's profile references hitting quarterly numbers but does not surface any specifics around dashboarding, funnel conversion analysis, time-to-fill benchmarking, or tools like Greenhouse, Workday, or Gem. For a company that runs on Foundry, this gap deserves direct investigation.

- **No visible exposure to go-to-market or business-side recruiting.** Her entire technical recruiting track record is in engineering functions. Palantir's Talent Strategist role spans engineering, deployment, *and* go-to-market teams. GTM recruiting — sales, business development, forward deployed roles — has a different candidate psychology, sourcing motion, and close strategy. Whether she can flex across both functions without a ramp period is untested.

## Interview probe questions

**1. Mission conviction — the hard question**
*"Palantir's work includes building systems used by intelligence agencies, military operations, and law enforcement. Walk me through how you've thought about that — not generally, but specifically. What parts of Palantir's mission did you engage with before this conversation, and where, if anywhere, do you have genuine tension with it?"*
> **Strong answer demonstrates:** Specific knowledge of Gotham, AIP, or Palantir's government contracts; an honest, considered perspective rather than a rehearsed endorsement; evidence that they sought out this context proactively rather than after seeing the job post.

---

**2. Sourcing strategy under constraint**
*"At Databricks, you were recruiting ML engineers in one of the most competitive markets in the decade. Walk me through a specific role where your initial sourcing strategy wasn't working — what the data told you, how you diagnosed the problem, and what you changed."*
> **Strong answer demonstrates:** Specific use of pipeline funnel data to identify where candidates were dropping; a concrete pivot in sourcing channel, message, or target profile; ownership of the outcome rather than attribution to market conditions.

---

**3. Stakeholder pushback and calibration**
*"Palantir hiring managers — especially on the engineering side — often come in with a profile that's either over-specified or internally inconsistent. Tell me about a time you pushed back on a hiring manager's role definition or candidate criteria. What was the disagreement, how did you make your case, and what happened?"*
> **Strong answer demonstrates:** A specific calibration conflict, not a generic "I work collaboratively with stakeholders" answer; evidence of using data or market intelligence to support the pushback; a result where the manager adjusted their thinking.

---

**4. GTM recruiting fluency**
*"This role covers engineering and go-to-market recruiting — and those are very different motions. What experience, if any, do you have recruiting for sales, business development, or customer-facing roles? If limited, walk me through how you'd approach sourcing and closing a senior enterprise account executive with SLED sector experience — what would your first two weeks on that search look like?"*
> **Strong answer demonstrates:** Honest self-assessment about the gap, if it exists; a structured and specific approach to an unfamiliar search, including where they'd start sourcing, how they'd build the candidate profile, and how they'd adapt their close strategy to a non-engineering mindset.

---

**5. Relocation conviction and compensation reality**
*"Our base range for this role is $90K–$120K with RSU upside. You're currently in San Francisco. Help me understand your thinking — what's driving the move, what's your timeline, and how are you thinking about the comp relative to your current situation and what you'd need in New York?"*
> **Strong answer demonstrates:** A specific and already-considered answer — not a vague "I'm open to it"; clarity on what they're currently earning and whether this band requires a step back; conviction about the move that isn't contingent solely on offer terms.
